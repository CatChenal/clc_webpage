Part II - Discussion

    Models of wild-type 3ORG
  1. M4T is the worst performer of the set. It used the 2R9H structure deposited in 2007 by Miller & Nguitragool, which is none other that a mutant of 1OTS with fab fragments (its descriptive title is "Crystal Structure of Q207C Mutant of CLC-ec1 in complex with Fab"). Moreover, it selected the same template structure when 3ORG was submitted. Therefore it is no wonder that it cannot model the CBS domain.
  2. ModWeb is odd in a different way: none of the 2 structures returned (#2 is shown) have the N-ter portion and #1 does not have the CBS domain. Model#2 has it, but it does not look right. Also the paucity of the information given with the model makes it easy to assimilate ModWeb to a blackbox.
  3. SWISS-MOD uses 3ORG as template (although it consistently chooses 3orgC, not 3orgA), however, it seems to stick to it too much and apparentlydiscarded the N-terminal segment that is part of the wt model.
  4. From the model of 3ORG returned by I-TASSER, I had high hopes that it could handle another 85 residues and modell the wild type just as well, but only the first model is useable. It is possible that another attempt will change this, for it seems the problem emanates from the template list: in the wt-ORG run, a very odd entry appears (even though it is not a homologue), so perhaps this may thrown the iterations off kilter.

Conclusion:

Three of the four modelling programs seem to have a sequence length threshold. While M4T does not have an outright input sequence length limit, unlike the SAM06 server whose limit is 600 residues, it does behave very oddly and is not to be relied on. The best is I-TASSER although it perform better on the shorter sequence.

Therefore, on the basis of this limited experiment, both hypotheses are proven wrong:

  1. In spite of the availability of several templates for the N-terminal modeling AND despite the fact that secondary structure forming helices ought to be readily identified, only the I-TSASSER programs was unable the recover this helical region.
  2. As per the picture of the best I-TASSER model below showing the 1OTS dimer overlayed with the full model, the N-terminal has no apparent interaction with the other monomer or with the CBS domain, although the ultimate test would be to refine the expression and purification procedures so that the exchanger can be characterized in its integral state.
